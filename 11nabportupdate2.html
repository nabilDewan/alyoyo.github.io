About Me

Born and raised in Shamshernagar, Sylhet, Bangladesh, I began my graduate journey in 2018 at Khulna University of Engineering and Technology (KUET), specializing in Industrial Engineering. Over four & half years, I gained hands-on expertise in supply chain optimization, digital tools, and operations management through research and real-world projects.

My academic contributions include four published research articles on sustainable & resilient supply chains and emerging technologies. Professionally, I worked as a Supply Chain Production Planning Engineer at Akij Plastics Ltd. Now, as a PhD student in Hydrogen Supply Chain at Warwick University (WMG), I am committed to advancing sustainability research while expanding my professional network and expertise.

Research Agenda

My research focuses on the intersection of supply chain management and technology, leveraging data analytics, business intelligence, and digital transformation to enhance overall efficiency, resilience, and sustainability. I explore how emerging technologies—such as blockchain, AI, ML, and IoT—can optimize real-time visibility, automation, and decision-making in complex supply chains. Additionally, I analyze barriers to technology adoption and develop strategic frameworks to drive innovation and adaptability in modern supply chain systems.
